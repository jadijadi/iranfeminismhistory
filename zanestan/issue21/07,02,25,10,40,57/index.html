Mehrangiz Kar Human Rights Lawyer and Women’s Rights Activist

Mehrangiz Kar: She is one of the internationally well-known Human Rights lawyer and Women’s Rights activist from Iran



She has born in 1944 in one of the small traditional southern town of Iran.
She has born in a very ordinary family.
In spite of all obstacles she studied Law in Tehran University and became a lawyer.
She discovered her writing ability as well and began to write on Women’s Rights issues before revolution



Soon, she became famous with her unique work.
She got married to a journalist in 1968.
The Islamic Revolution of Iran in 1979 changed her life and her destiny.


Despite all dangers she chose to stay in Iran and continued her work and did not leave her country.
Soon after revolution the secular legal system in Iran shifted to a non-secular one. This system was based on fundamental understanding of Islam.
So many discriminatory rules and punishment have been defined in this new system.
She observed all these changes and got more motivation to stand for Iranian women’s right.

She realized that this new established system would not be change overnight again.
In spite of her non-religion background, she started to study Shari’a law to be able to make a dialogue with the legal system in their religious language.
After a decade of silence, she started her writing again in a very conservative and soft way about women’s right under so much pressures and minimum of freedom of speech and thought.
She was the first person who took this risk and talked about women’s right publicly after revolution in Iran.


Soon, she got famous among Iranian as a heroine and also international human rights organization and western academia valued her works as well.
She was the voice of Iranian women’s rights activists.
The fundamentals could not tolerate even this much of critics and threaten her to dead.
They could not arrest her easily as she had an international name and support.
But finally in March 2000 upon her arrival from a conference in Germany, she has been arrested and jailed because of her activities.

With supporting the international human rights organizations, she has been released under a huge bail.
But soon after, she has been diagnosed with an aggressive breast cancer which was developed in jail.
Medical team gave her hope of life only for couple of years.
She got more energy to work. during her chemotherapy she wrote two valuable books about Women’s rights issues in Iran.
Finally, the government permitted her to leave the country to abroad for getting better treatments

She arrived to Washington DC. Where she supposed to stay only for few months.
After two months, her husband has been kidnapped in Iran. So, she could not go back again till now.
She did not give up and started to work with Human Rights organizations and Universities in the States to promote women’s right in Iran.
She taught Women Rights in Islam in several universities in the States during last 6 years.
She is the writer of 15 books and hundreds of articles all about human rights and women’s rights in Iran.
She is working with Human Rights center of Harvard University for last three years


She is the winner of so many Human Rights international prizes such as, Human Rights award of European Union Bar Association in 2002, Human rights first award in 2004.


Her energy source is coming from her pure belief and her true understanding of necessity of promoting women’s rights and her commitment to Human Rights principles.
She always gave energy and idea to women Iranian women’s rights activists and led them to challenge the discriminatory rules with emphasizing on new interpretation of religion.
She also always gain back the energy from tasting the result of her work even if they are too small and slow.
That is why she survived all the disasters and still working so hard.
